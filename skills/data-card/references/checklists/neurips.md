# NeurIPS Reproducibility Checklist (main track)

Auto-fill each question with YES / NO / N/A + free-text justification
with section/page pointers.

## Claims and motivation
1. Do the main claims made in the abstract and introduction
   accurately reflect the paper's contributions and scope?
2. Did you describe the limitations of your work?
3. Did you discuss any potential negative societal impacts of your
   work?
4. Have you read the ethics review guidelines and ensured that your
   paper conforms to them?

## Theory
5. For all theoretical results, did you state the full set of
   assumptions of all theoretical results?
6. Did you include complete proofs of all theoretical results?

## Experiments
7. Did you include the code, data, and instructions needed to
   reproduce the main experimental results?
8. Did you specify all the training details (e.g., data splits,
   hyperparameters, how they were chosen)?
9. Did you report error bars (e.g., with respect to the random seed
   after running experiments multiple times)?
10. Did you include the total amount of compute and the type of
    resources used (e.g., type of GPUs, internal cluster, or cloud
    provider)?

## Existing assets
11. If your work uses existing assets, did you cite the creators?
12. Did you mention the license of the assets?
13. Did you include any new assets either in the supplemental material
    or as a URL?
14. Did you discuss whether and how consent was obtained from people
    whose data you're using/curating?

## Crowdsourcing and human subjects (if applicable)
15. Did you include the full text of instructions given to
    participants and screenshots, if applicable?
16. Did you describe any potential participant risks, with links to
    Institutional Review Board (IRB) approvals, if applicable?
17. Did you include the estimated hourly wage paid to participants
    and the total amount spent on participant compensation?
